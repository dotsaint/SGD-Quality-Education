Quality Education is the fourth goal of the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), established in 2015 as part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. It focuses on ensuring inclusive, equitable, and quality education for all and promoting lifelong learning opportunities.

Key Targets of SDG 4

1. Free Primary and Secondary Education

Ensure all boys and girls complete free, equitable, and quality primary and secondary education leading to relevant and effective learning outcomes.

2. Early Childhood Development and Pre-Primary Education

Ensure access to quality early childhood development, care, and pre-primary education to prepare children for primary education.

4. Equal Access to Technical and Higher Education

Ensure equal access for all to affordable and quality technical, vocational, and tertiary education, including university.

3. Skills for Employment

Substantially increase the number of youth and adults who have relevant skills for employment, decent jobs, and entrepreneurship.

6. Universal Literacy and Numeracy

Ensure all youth and most adults achieve literacy and numeracy.

5. Gender Equality in Education

Eliminate gender disparities in education and ensure equal access for all, especially for vulnerable populations, including persons with disabilities and indigenous peoples.
